Market Equilibrium

Definition and Key Properties

  • The market equilibrium is the price-quantity pair $(P,Q)$ such that supply and demand are equal, $Q = Q^{S}(P) = Q^{D}(P)$.
  • Geometrically, the market equilibrium is the intersection of the supply and demand curves.
  • At the market equilibrium, there is no excess demand nor excess supply.
  • If the price is above (below) the market equilibrium price, there is excess supply (demand), and the price tends to decrease (increase).
  • The price adjustment process. Classroom experiment using the Virginia Econ Lab.
  • The market equilibrium maximizes the total surplus (consumer plus producer surpluses).
